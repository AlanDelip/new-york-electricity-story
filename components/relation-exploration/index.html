What is affecting the electricity use?
Relation Exploration
Now that we've noticed some visual patterns from the calendar view, whether the electricity load will actually be influenced by them is to be validated in a more logical way.
Starting from the 6 categorized types will be a great approach.
Here let's try to transform dots in the calendar view into an axis system using x-position to indicate categories and y-position to indicate the consumption.

Natural factors win from the first glance.

Since the temperature has the biggest visual effect on the electricity load, we're now trying to use temperature as the x-axis to separate the factor and see more details.
Hover on one of the labels, we can see corresponding data in a highlighted form.
Focusing on temperature only, we can find out that both high temperature and low temperature have a positive relation with the electricity load. What's more, a high-temperature situation stands out more significantly.
Focusing on weekends and normal days (weekdays without other conditions), it's clearly illustrated that weekends mostly distribute below the normal days' boundary despite the temperature factor.
The short rest for people and their companies contributes to the reduction on electricity usage.
While answering the 'What' question, part of the answer to 'How' is also revealed.
Both natural(temperature) and human(weekends) factors contribute to the abnormality in electricity consumption. For the temperature factor, the electricity usage tends to go higher when it gets closer to either end of its extremes(cold or hot).
